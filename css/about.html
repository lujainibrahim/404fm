About me

I'm currently living in Abu Dhabi and conducting research on human decision-making at the Laboratory for Computer-Human Intelligence at NYU Abu Dhabi led by Prof. Tuka Alhanai.

I recently graduated (online) from NYU Abu Dhabi where I spoke on behalf of my class. At NYU Abu Dhabi, I studied computer engineering and completed my senior year thesis on explainable machine learning for the early detection of heart attacks. My research interests are primarly in developing explainable & trustworthy AI applications for tackling pressing health & human behavior issues.

I'm also interested in learning about and interrogating the societal implications of AI and the internet. I'm currently a part of a team of volunteer organizers planning this year's Mozilla Foundation festival (MozFest) on trustworthy AI. Last summer, I co-founded Multiplicity, a digital curation examining the global impact of the internet on young people's lives, with my classmate and friend Alia Elkattan. In addition to running and building Multiplicity's website, I wrote an article on digital exile, responding to Edward Said's essay Reflections on Exile with my own experience of growing up on the internet & in exile.

Finally, I'm a film enthusiast! I've written about all sorts of films academically and extracurricularly. Most recently, I've co-curated a film issue on "Unequal States" with over 30 films documenting global inequality for the science-film platform Labocine.
